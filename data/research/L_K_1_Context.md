# Kindergarten ELA Standard L.K.1 Context & Constraints

## 1. Core Pedagogical Boundaries
For Kindergarten (Grade 0) ELA Grammar and Conventions (L.K.1):
* **Do NOT test Subject-Verb Agreement**: Distinguishing between singular and plural verbs (e.g., "boy run" vs "boy runs") is a Grade 1 standard (L.1.1). Do not ask the student to choose between present tense conjugations like "run", "runs", "running", or "runned".
* **Do NOT include irrelevant sentences**: Do not add extra introductory or distracting sentences (such as "God is kind." or "The game is fun.") unless it is directly part of the extremely short question text.

## 2. Permitted Target Skills for Kindergarten
Only generate questions testing one of the following simple, concrete skills:
1. **Identifying Action Words (Verbs) or Naming Words (Nouns)**:
   * Example Passage: "See the dog run."
   * Question: "Which word is an action word?"
   * Correct: "run"
   * Distractors: "the", "dog", "see"
2. **Plural Nouns (adding -s)**:
   * Example: "One dog. Two ____."
   * Question: "Which word fits?"
   * Correct: "dogs"
   * Distractors: "dog", "cat", "run"
3. **Uppercase vs Lowercase Letter Identification**:
   * Example: "Find the big capital letter."
   * Question: "Which is a capital letter?"
   * Correct: "T"
   * Distractors: "t", "a", "c"

## 3. Ultra-Strict Vocabulary and Length Limits
* **Passage length**: Exactly 1 short sentence (maximum 4-5 words).
* **Vocabulary**: Concrete, high-frequency sight words and CVC (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant) words only (e.g., "dog", "cat", "run", "big", "sun", "see", "ball", "box").
