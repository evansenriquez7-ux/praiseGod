# Standard HSG-CO.A.1 Context & Generation Constraints

## Node Description
HSG-CO.A.1: Know precise definitions of angle, circle, perpendicular line, parallel line, and line segment, based on the undefined notions of point, line, distance along a line, and distance around a circular arc.

## Generation Constraints
- **Grade Level:** High School (Geometry)
- **Vocabulary:** Precise geometric terminology. Use standard notation (e.g., ray AB, segment CD, intersecting).
- **Constraints:** Questions must avoid relying purely on intuition or visual estimation; they must test the *rigorous* mathematical definition. For example, testing that parallel lines must be coplanar.
- **Distractors:** Use colloquial definitions (e.g., "lines that never touch" without specifying coplanar) as traps for imprecise thinking.

## Sample Practice Problems

### Problem 1 (Precise Definition of Parallel Lines)
**Stem:** Which of the following is the most precise mathematical definition of parallel lines?
**Options:**
- (A) Two lines in the same plane that do not intersect. (Correct)
- (B) Two lines that never touch each other, no matter how far they are extended. (Trap: Fails to specify they must be coplanar, which would allow skew lines)
- (C) Two lines that are always the exact same distance apart. (Trap: True property, but not the foundational definition based on undefined terms)
- (D) Two lines in a plane that cross at a 90-degree angle. (Trap: Definition of perpendicular)

### Problem 2 (Precise Definition of a Circle)
**Stem:** Based on undefined notions of point and distance, what defines a circle?
**Options:**
- (A) The set of all points in a plane that are at a given distance from a given point. (Correct)
- (B) A perfectly round 2D shape with no corners. (Trap: Colloquial, non-rigorous)
- (C) The set of points that form an arc measuring 360 degrees. (Trap: Circular logic / uses derived angle measure)
- (D) A curved line that connects back to itself without intersecting. (Trap: Vague, applies to any closed loop)
