# Kindergarten Mathematical Practice (MP) Standards - Implementation & Examples

## Overview
The 8 Mathematical Practices are "habits of mind" rather than rote calculable skills. Standard multiple-choice generation (like SymPy equation solving) is not appropriate for these nodes at the Kindergarten level. Instead, these nodes are assessed using "Meta-Math" or Error Analysis questions.

## Reference Examples for Implementation

### MP.1: Make Sense of Problems and Persevere in Solving Them
**Concept:** Can the student make sense of what the problem is asking before jumping to numbers? Do they choose the right model?
**Kindergarten Implementation Strategy:** "Choose the Model" or "Story Matching".
*   **Sample Question:** "There are 5 birds on a branch. 2 fly away. Which picture shows how we find out how many are left?"
*   **Options:** 
    *   (A) A picture of 7 birds.
    *   (B) A picture of 3 birds. (Correct)
    *   (C) A picture of 2 birds.

### MP.2: Reason Abstractly and Quantitatively
**Concept:** Moving between an abstract number (the digit '7') and a concrete quantity (7 blocks).
**Kindergarten Implementation Strategy:** "Abstract to Quantity" mapping.
*   **Sample Question:** "Which number tells how many stars are in this box?" (Image/context of 8 stars).
*   **Options:** 
    *   (A) 6 
    *   (B) 8 (Correct)
    *   (C) 10

### MP.3: Construct Viable Arguments and Critique the Reasoning of Others
**Concept:** Error analysis. Can they spot a mistake someone else made?
**Kindergarten Implementation Strategy:** "Spot the Mistake" or "Critique the Character".
*   **Sample Question:** "Sam says that 2 + 2 is 5. He shows his work with these dots: [● ●] and [● ● ●]. Did Sam make a mistake?"
*   **Options:** 
    *   (A) No, Sam is right. 
    *   (B) Yes, Sam put too many dots in the second group. (Correct)
    *   (C) Yes, Sam should have used squares.

### MP.4: Model with Mathematics
**Concept:** Applying math to everyday situations.
**Kindergarten Implementation Strategy:** "Real-World Context Matching".
*   **Sample Question:** "You have 3 apples and you buy 2 more. Which math sentence shows what happened?"
*   **Options:**
    *   (A) 3 + 2 = 5 (Correct)
    *   (B) 3 - 2 = 1
    *   (C) 5 - 3 = 2

### MP.5: Use Appropriate Tools Strategically
**Concept:** Selecting the right tool for the job.
**Kindergarten Implementation Strategy:** "Which Tool?"
*   **Sample Question:** "You want to find out how long your desk is. Which tool should you use?"
*   **Options:**
    *   (A) A scale
    *   (B) A ruler (Correct)
    *   (C) A clock

### MP.6: Attend to Precision
**Concept:** Being precise with language and counting.
**Kindergarten Implementation Strategy:** "Precision Vocabulary".
*   **Sample Question:** "Look at this shape with 3 straight sides. What is the BEST name for it?"
*   **Options:**
    *   (A) A pointy shape
    *   (B) A triangle (Correct)
    *   (C) A square

### MP.7: Look for and Make Use of Structure
**Concept:** Recognizing patterns and structures (e.g., sorting, grouping by 10s).
**Kindergarten Implementation Strategy:** "Pattern Recognition" or "Grouping".
*   **Sample Question:** "If we put these 10 red blocks and 10 blue blocks together, how many groups of 10 do we have?"
*   **Options:**
    *   (A) 1 group
    *   (B) 2 groups (Correct)
    *   (C) 20 groups

### MP.8: Look for and Express Regularity in Repeated Reasoning
**Concept:** Noticing when calculations are repeated (e.g., adding 1 is just the next number).
**Kindergarten Implementation Strategy:** "Shortcut / Rule Identification".
*   **Sample Question:** "When you add 1 to a number, you just say the next number. What is 8 + 1?"
*   **Options:**
    *   (A) 9 (Correct)
    *   (B) 7
    *   (C) 10
