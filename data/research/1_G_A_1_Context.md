# Standard 1.G.A.1 Context & Generation Constraints

## Node Description
1.G.A.1: Distinguish between defining attributes (e.g., triangles are closed and three-sided) versus non-defining attributes (e.g., color, orientation, overall size); build and draw shapes to possess defining attributes.

## Generation Constraints
- **Grade Level:** 1st Grade
- **Vocabulary:** Simple, decodable text. Use words like "shape", "sides", "corners", "color", "size".
- **Focus:** The questions must test whether a student knows what makes a shape what it is (sides/corners) vs what doesn't matter (color/size/direction).
- **Distractors:** Always include color or size as a trap option.

## Sample Practice Problems

### Problem 1 (Defining Attributes)
**Stem:** What makes this shape a triangle? (Image of a big red triangle)
**Options:**
- (A) It has 3 sides and 3 corners. (Correct)
- (B) It is red. (Trap: Non-defining attribute)
- (C) It is big. (Trap: Non-defining attribute)
- (D) It has 4 sides. (Trap: Wrong defining attribute)

### Problem 2 (Non-Defining Attributes)
**Stem:** Look at these two squares. One is blue and one is yellow. Are they both squares?
**Options:**
- (A) Yes, because color does not change the shape. (Correct)
- (B) No, because one is blue. (Trap: Misunderstanding non-defining attribute)
- (C) No, because they are different. (Trap: Vague, incorrect)
- (D) Yes, because they are both blue. (Trap: Incorrect premise)
